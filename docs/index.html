Blackjack

How to play

Goal

The goal is to have a hand value higher than the dealer's hand, without exceeding 21.

Card Values

- Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.

- Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10.

- Aces can be worth either 1 or 11, depending on what's more advantageous for the hand.

Player actions

Hit

The player requests an additional card from the dealer. If, when requesting a card, the player obtains a score equal to 21, they automatically stand.

Bet

The player can bet any amount they want from the total money they have available.

Stand

The player chooses to stop taking cards and ends their turn.

Double Down

Double the initial bet in exchange for receiving exactly one more card and then standing, regardless of the card's value.

Insurance Bet

Check insurance in special situations section.

Round result

Win

Player must have a hand value higher than the dealer's hand, without exceeding 21. Standard hand pays out 1:1, but the payout for blackjack is 3:2 (see special situations section).

Bust

The player exceeds a total value of 21

Push

The player and the dealer have the same value for their hands at the end of a round and the player's original bet is returned.

Special situations

Blackjack

Is a hand consisting of an Ace and any 10-point card (10, Jack, Queen, or King) dealt as the first two cards. It is the highest possible hand in the game and the player automatically wins unless the dealer also has a blackjack.

Insurance

Is a side bet that the dealer has a blackjack. The dealer asks for insurance bets before the first player plays. Insurance bets of up to half the player's current bet are placed on the "insurance bar" above the player's cards. If the dealer has a blackjack, insurance pays 2 to 1.
