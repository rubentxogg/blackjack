Blackjack

How to play

Goal

The goal is to have a hand value higher than the dealer's hand, without exceeding 21.

Card Values

- Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.

- Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10.

- Aces can be worth either 1 or 11, depending on what's more advantageous for the hand.

Player actions

Bet

The player can bet any amount they want from the total money they have available.

Hit

The player requests an additional card from the dealer. This is done when the player wants to improve their hand total, hoping to get closer to 21 without going over (busting).

Stand

The player chooses to stop taking cards and ends their turn. This is done when the player is satisfied with their hand total or believes they are in a strong position to beat the dealer.

Round result

Win

Player must have a hand value higher than the dealer's hand, without exceeding 21. A winning hand in blackjack pays out at a ratio of 1 to 1 (1:1), but if the player wins with a blackjack, the payout is 3:2.

Bust

player or the dealer's hand exceeds a total value of 21

Push

Occurs when the player and the dealer have the same total value for their hands at the end of a round. When this happens, the player neither wins nor loses the bet; instead, the player's original bet is returned.

Special situations

Blackjack

Is a hand consisting of an Ace and any 10-point card (10, Jack, Queen, or King) dealt as the first two cards. It is the highest possible hand in the game and the player automatically wins unless the dealer also has a blackjack.
